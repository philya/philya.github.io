Some of the more recent projects I worked on:

Scientist “Migration”
2019
Prophy.Science

Prophy.Science aggregates and analyzes a majority of the English-language scientific literature published in the world today. They do a unique job of aggregating, parsing, cataloging, and classifying papers retrieved in a variety of formats and from hundreds of sources. This work allows them to accumulate a trove of metadata information on the trends and history of scientific research.

In this project I looked at the publishing scientists’ affiliated institutions, and how these changed over time, in order to identify the “migration” patterns of science workers across countries.

Business goal: Engage scientists and science journalists on reddit and other social media platforms.

Tech: PostgreSQL, BigQuery, Python, SVG

University’s Top Collaborations
2019
Prophy.Science

Scientific papers are often written by multiple co-authors, or collaborators. Such collaborators may be affiliated with different institutions located in different countries. This map outlines X university’s top scientific collaborations, visually grouped by the corresponding institutions and countries. Top collaborations are selected by the H-Index of the collaboration.

Business goal: Provide useful information to a prospective customer—the university administration. This visualization was part of an outreach campaign aimed at large European universities.

Tech: PostgreSQL, BigQuery, Python, HTML

Trending Topics for Taboola Trends
2017–2018
Taboola, Inc.

Online advertising giant Taboola reaches more than one billion internet users through its widgets on various partner sites. The company records a lot of anonymized data: what pages users visit, when, how often, and for how long. I looked into the dataset to find how different news topics are connected by common readership. To present the results I created an interactive graph (network) visualization of the relationships. The project eventually evolved into the Trending Topics section on the Taboola Trends website.

Business goal: The Trending Topics section and its related intranet products are frequently referred to by industry professionals, the company’s partners, and Taboola employees.

Tech: BigQuery, Python, D3.js

Learn more: https://trends.taboola.com
